01 / About

Movement is one of the earliest windows into brain development.

I work at the intersection of computer vision, deep learning, and clinical assessment, building tools that detect early markers of neurodevelopmental disorders from infant video. The core idea across my projects: pose and movement, extracted at the joint level, can be mapped onto established clinical frameworks — General Movement Assessment (Prechtl), HINE, and AIMS — rather than treated as an opaque classification target.

Recent work focuses on getting the statistics right, not just the architecture: correcting spurious correlations on small validation sets, moving to leave-one-out cross-validation where sample sizes demand it, and checking inter-rater agreement before multi-reviewer clinical scores are ever averaged together.

Based in Tamil Nadu, India — building primarily in Google Colab, with an eye toward IEEE and journal publication.

02 / Research

Current pipelines

Three active lines of work, each addressing a different piece of video-based neurodevelopmental screening.

Pose Graph Network

Infant-GCN-PSN

A pose similarity network built on MediaPipe joint extraction and clinician-annotated ground truth. Version 3 rebuilds the statistical layer: minimum-sample thresholds, automatic routing to LOOCV on small cohorts, and inter-rater checks before composite scoring.

Statusv3 in progress
ST-GAT

InfantNDD-STGAT

A three-channel Spatio-Temporal Graph Attention Network over 58 biomechanical features across 21 joints, with output heads aligned to GMA, HINE, and AIMS, fused into a single 0–100 Neurodevelopmental Movement Biomarker Index.

EvaluationBCa bootstrap · DeLong's
Medical Imaging

SUSMI Challenge

A lightweight pneumonia diagnosis pipeline from chest X-rays using EfficientNet-B0 transfer learning under a strict parameter budget, interpreted with Grad-CAM, SHAP, and LIME.

ArchitectureEfficientNet-B0
